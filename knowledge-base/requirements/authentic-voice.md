# Authentic Voice & Style Guidelines

This knowledge base replaces the Chinese "anti-AI" detection requirements. Its goal is to ensure the generated text sounds human, authentic, and engaging, avoiding the sterile or overly melodramatic hallmarks of default AI writing.

## Core Rules for Authentic Prose

1. **Vary Sentence Structure**
   - AI naturally falls into a rhythm of medium-length sentences with similar structures. Break this. Use very short sentences for impact. Use longer, flowing sentences for introspection or description.
   - Avoid starting consecutive sentences with the same pronoun or structure (e.g., "He looked. He walked. He thought.").

2. **Show, Don't Tell (With Specificity)**
   - **Bad:** "She was very sad about the news."
   - **Better:** "She stared at the phone screen until the screen went dark, her thumb hovering over the 'call' button."
   - Focus on micro-actions and specific, grounded physical sensations rather than abstract emotions.

3. **Avoid Melodrama and Clichés**
   - Do NOT use phrases like: "shivered down her spine," "let out a breath he didn't know he was holding," "eyes widened in shock," or "heart hammered in his chest."
   - Humans react in complex, sometimes understated ways. A character might react to devastating news by carefully folding a napkin, rather than screaming.

4. **Dialogue Must Have Subtext**
   - People rarely say exactly what they mean. They talk around the issue, deflect, lie, or use sarcasm.
   - Limit dialogue tags. If it's clear who is speaking, drop the "he said." Never use tags like "she exclaimed dramatically." Use action beats instead.

5. **Sensory Grounding**
   - Ground scenes in at least two non-visual senses. The smell of ozone, the grit of dust under fingernails, the oppressive heat of the room.

6. **Flawed Perspectives**
   - The narrative voice should be filtered through the POV character. Their observations should reflect their biases, background, and current emotional state, not an objective camera lens.
