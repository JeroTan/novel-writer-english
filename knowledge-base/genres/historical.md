# Historical Genre Knowledge

## Core Conventions
1. **Authenticity over Accuracy:** Readers want the *feel* of the era. Historical accuracy is important, but emotional authenticity and relatable character motivations matter more.
2. **The Setting as a Character:** The societal rules of the time (class structures, gender roles, technological limitations) must actively constrain and challenge the protagonist.
3. **Pacing:** Often slower, allowing for immersive descriptions of clothing, architecture, and daily life.
4. **Common Tropes:** Court intrigue, arranged marriages, social climbing, war/rebellion.

## Tropes to Subvert or Execute Well
- The "modern person" in a historical setting (ensure their modern sensibilities cause realistic friction).
- The secret heir.
