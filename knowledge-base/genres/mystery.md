# Mystery Genre Conventions

This knowledge base provides guidance for writing Mystery novels.
Mystery revolves around a puzzle—usually a crime—that the protagonist and the reader try to solve together.

## Core Elements
1. **The Crime:** Usually a murder, occurring early in the story to kick off the plot.
2. **The Detective:** The protagonist trying to solve the crime. Can be an amateur (Cozy Mystery) or professional (Police Procedural / Noir).
3. **Clues and Red Herrings:** Genuine pieces of evidence mixed with false leads designed to distract the detective and the reader.
4. **The Reveal:** The climax where the detective explains how the crime was committed and exposes the culprit.

## Reader Expectations
- **Fair Play:** The reader must have access to all the clues necessary to solve the mystery before the detective reveals the answer. Do not introduce a completely new character or clue in the final chapter.
- **Logic:** The solution must make logical sense based on the established facts.
- **Pacing:** The investigation must constantly move forward. Every chapter should reveal a new clue, suspect, or twist.

## Common Pitfalls
- **The "Idiot" Police:** Making the professional investigators unrealistically incompetent just to make the amateur detective look smart.
- **Coincidence Solving the Case:** The detective shouldn't stumble upon the confession tape purely by accident. They must use deduction and active investigation.
