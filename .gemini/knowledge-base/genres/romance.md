# Romance Genre Conventions

This knowledge base provides guidance for writing Romance novels.
The core of romance is the development of a romantic relationship and an emotionally satisfying ending.

## Core Elements
1. **The Meet-Cute:** The initial encounter between the protagonists. It sets the tone for their dynamic.
2. **The Conflict (Internal & External):** What keeps them apart? External (war, family feud, distance) and Internal (fear of commitment, past trauma).
3. **The Climax/Dark Moment:** The "All is Lost" moment where it seems the relationship will fail.
4. **The HEA/HFN:** "Happily Ever After" or "Happy For Now". Without this, it is not a romance novel; it's a tragedy or general fiction.

## Common Tropes
- Enemies to Lovers
- Friends to Lovers
- Fake Dating / Marriage of Convenience
- Second Chance Romance
- Grumpy x Sunshine

## Reader Expectations
- **Emotional Resonance:** The reader must feel the connection. Show the chemistry through dialogue, body language, and internal thoughts.
- **Focus on the Couple:** Even if there is a subplot (a mystery, a war), the relationship must be the primary driver of the narrative.
- **The Payoff:** The resolution of the romantic tension must feel earned, not rushed.

## Common Pitfalls
- **Lack of Communication as the ONLY Conflict:** If the entire plot could be resolved by a 5-minute conversation, readers will get frustrated. Provide valid reasons for the lack of communication, or use other conflicts.
- **Unearned Forgiveness:** If one character betrays the other terribly, they must work hard to earn forgiveness. It cannot happen instantly.
