# Strong Emotion Guidelines

## Purpose
To ensure scenes land with maximum emotional impact, whether that's grief, rage, joy, or terror.

## Core Rules
1. **The Objective Correlative:** Use physical objects or the environment to mirror the character's internal emotional state.
2. **Physicality:** Describe how the emotion physically affects the body before naming the emotion itself (e.g., "His vision narrowed to a pinprick, hands shaking" vs "He felt intensely angry").
3. **Pacing the Peak:** Build up to the emotional climax slowly. Don't rush the realization or the breakdown. Let the character (and the reader) sit in the feeling.
